How much will my tag and registration cost in Idaho?

For most Idaho drivers, the annual registration fee for a standard passenger car is very affordable, ranging from $45 to $69 depending entirely on the age of the vehicle. However, your total out-of-pocket cost at the DMV will also include local county administrative fees, which vary by location. If you live in Ada County (Boise), you will also pay a local highway district fee. Fully electric vehicles and plug-in hybrids face additional flat surcharges to offset state gas taxes.

How are fees calculated in Idaho?

Unlike states that tax based on a vehicle's MSRP or its curb weight, Idaho simplifies passenger vehicle registration by using three flat age tiers:

  • 1 to 2 years old: $69.00
  • 3 to 6 years old: $57.00
  • 7 years and older: $45.00

On top of this base fee, every registration includes a $1.25 EMS (Emergency Medical Services) fee. Individual counties then attach their own administrative fees for registration and titling.

What affects your registration in Idaho?

  • Vehicle Age: The primary factor for passenger cars. The older your car gets, the less it costs to register.
  • Vehicle Type: Motorcycles ($25) and utility trailers ($5) have low flat rates, while heavy commercial trucks pay based on gross vehicle weight.
  • County of Residence: Each county sets its own administrative fees (ranging from $3.00 to $14.00). Ada County also assesses a dedicated highway district fee based on vehicle age.
  • Fuel Type: Fully electric vehicles (EVs) pay a $140 annual surcharge. Plug-in hybrids (PHEVs) pay $75.

Frequently asked questions

How much is the car registration fee in Idaho?

For a standard passenger car, the state fee is $69 for vehicles 1-2 years old, $57 for 3-6 years old, and $45 for vehicles 7 years or older. Local county admin and highway fees typically add about $10 to $50 to this base.

Is this calculator accurate for all vehicles in Idaho?

It is highly accurate for standard passenger vehicles, motorcycles, school buses, utility trailers, and standard trucks up to 60,000 lbs. Specialized commercial vehicles, RV motorhomes, and ATVs have different fee schedules and valuations.

Can registration fees in Idaho change every year?

Yes, but usually in your favor! Because Idaho uses an age-based tier system for passenger cars, your base registration fee will drop when your car turns 3 years old, and again when it turns 7 years old.

Can I use this calculator for a new car and a used car in Idaho?

Yes. Simply select the correct "Vehicle Age Band" and mark whether you need a new title transaction or new metal plates. The calculator will automatically adjust the base fees to match the used car's age bracket.
