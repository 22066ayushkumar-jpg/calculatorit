The Nevada vehicle registration fees calculator gives you a fast, mobile-first estimate of what you'll owe the Nevada DMV in 2026. Nevada charges a weight-based registration fee, a 4% Governmental Services Tax (GST) on a depreciated DMV valuation equal to 35% of your vehicle's original MSRP, and — in Clark, Washoe, and Churchill counties — an additional 1% Supplemental GST. On a dealer purchase you also pay state and local sales tax (6.85%–8.375%), a title fee, plate fee, lien recording ($20 per lien), and an optional $2 VIN inspection for out-of-state titles. How fees are calculated in Nevada

Nevada stacks several charges under NRS 371. Registration is a flat fee tied to vehicle weight class. The GST equals 4% of a depreciated DMV valuation, where valuation starts at 35% of MSRP, drops 5% in year 2, and 10% each year after, floored at 15%. A minimum GST of $16 applies. The optional SGST adds 1% in three counties. For dealer purchases, taxable amount = purchase price − trade-in and rebates, multiplied by your county's combined sales tax rate.

What affects your registration in Nevada?

  • MSRP. Higher MSRP means a higher DMV valuation and a higher GST/SGST bill.
  • Vehicle age. Depreciation reduces GST each year until the 15% floor.
  • County. Clark, Washoe, and Churchill add 1% Supplemental GST; Clark also has the highest 8.375% sales tax.
  • Weight class. Heavier vehicles pay higher base registration.
  • Vehicle type. Motorcycles add a $6 safety fee; trailers and mopeds have their own flat fees.
  • Title, plate, lien, and VIN inspection choices apply once at initial registration.

What this calculator may not include

  • Federal Heavy Vehicle Use Tax (HVUT) for trucks over 55,000 lbs GVW.

Frequently asked questions

Is this calculator accurate for all vehicles in Nevada?

It covers the vast majority of passenger cars, motorcycles, light trucks, mopeds, and travel trailers registered under NRS 371 using 2026 Nevada DMV rates. Commercial IRP-apportioned vehicles, farm plates, and specialty exemptions can fall outside standard schedules — verify those with the Nevada DMV.

How much does it cost to renew vehicle registration in Nevada?

A typical passenger renewal is the $33 base registration + the 4% GST on your depreciated DMV valuation (minimum $16) + 1% SGST if you live in Clark, Washoe, or Churchill county. Most renewals fall between $80 and $500 depending on MSRP and county.

How is the Nevada vehicle registration fee calculated?

Nevada combines a weight-based registration fee, a 4% Governmental Services Tax on a depreciated DMV valuation equal to 35% of MSRP (5% depreciation in year 2, then 10%/yr to a 15% floor), an optional 1% Supplemental GST in some counties, and one-time title, plate, lien, and VIN inspection fees. Sales tax (6.85%–8.375%) applies to dealer purchases only.
