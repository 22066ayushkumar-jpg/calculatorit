How much will my tag and registration cost in Georgia?

If you are simply renewing a passenger vehicle that already has a Georgia plate, the cost is astonishingly low: just a flat $20 per year. However, if you are buying a car or registering one for the first time in Georgia, you must pay the Title Ad Valorem Tax (TAVT). For a standard vehicle valued at $25,000, the 7% TAVT equates to a $1,750 upfront tax, plus an $18 title fee and the $20 tag fee. Fully electric vehicles (BEVs) also carry a significant annual alternative fuel surcharge (currently over $235 per year) to offset the lack of state fuel taxes.

How fees are calculated in Georgia

Georgia simplified its vehicle taxes in 2013, replacing the state sales tax and the ongoing annual property tax with a single upfront tax for most vehicles:

  • TAVT (Title Ad Valorem Tax): Most vehicles are assessed a one-time 7.0% tax based on their Fair Market Value (FMV). New residents moving into Georgia pay a discounted 3.0% rate. Immediate family transfers are taxed at just 0.5%.
  • Annual Ad Valorem Tax: Vehicles purchased before March 1, 2013, remain on the old system, paying annual property taxes based on local county millage rates applied to 40% of the vehicle’s assessed value.
  • Base Tag Fee: An annual flat fee of $20 applies to standard passenger cars and motorcycles.
  • Alternative Fuel Surcharge: A mandatory annual fee for fully electric vehicles (BEVs) and AFV-plated plug-in hybrids, which is adjusted yearly for inflation.

What affects your registration in Georgia?

  • Fair Market Value: Determines the bulk of your first-year registration cost by driving the TAVT calculation.
  • Transaction Type: Standard purchases pay 7% TAVT. However, out-of-state transplants pay a discounted 3% to prevent aggressive double-taxation.
  • Fuel Type: Fully electric vehicles (BEVs) owe the annual AFV surcharge (>$235), whereas standard gas and non-plug-in hybrid cars do not.
  • Location: If you live in one of the 13 Metro Atlanta counties, most gas-powered vehicles require a passed emissions test, which typically adds up to $25 to your overall costs.

Frequently asked questions

How much is the average car registration in Georgia?

Once the initial Title Ad Valorem Tax (TAVT) is paid, the average car registration renewal in Georgia is just $20 per year for standard gas vehicles. Electric vehicles average around $255 per year due to the mandatory alternative fuel surcharge.

Is this calculator accurate for all vehicles in Georgia?

It is highly accurate for standard passenger cars, motorcycles, and light trucks. Commercial trucks, apportioned vehicles, and vehicles running on commercial alternative fuels like compressed natural gas have different statutory fee formulas.

Can registration fees in Georgia change every year?

The $20 base tag fee and the 7% TAVT rate are historically stable. However, the annual Alternative Fuel Vehicle (EV) fee is statutorily indexed to fuel efficiencies and inflation, meaning it changes every July.

Can I use this calculator for a new car and a used car in Georgia?

Yes. Georgia applies the exact same 7% Title Ad Valorem Tax (TAVT) to both new and used vehicles based on their Fair Market Value. Just enter the fair market value of the vehicle to calculate your one-time tax and registration cost.
