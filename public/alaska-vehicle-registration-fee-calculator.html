The Alaska vehicle registration fees calculator gives you a fast, transparent estimate of what you will owe the Alaska DMV at your next renewal. Alaska uses a mostly flat state registration fee — typically $100 biennial for a passenger car — combined with a Motor Vehicle Registration Tax (MVRT) collected on behalf of your borough or city. That MVRT starts high on newer vehicles and depreciates each year based on the local rate schedule, and it varies significantly between Anchorage, Mat-Su, Juneau, Kenai, and smaller flat-rate communities. EV owners add a biennial surcharge under HB 104, and Z-Tab permanent registration is available for older vehicles.

How much will my tag and registration cost in Alaska?

Most Alaska drivers pay between $100 and $300 every two years for a standard passenger car. A brand-new SUV in Anchorage or Juneau can push the total past $400 because MVRT is highest in the first year. A 10-year-old sedan in a flat-rate community like Sitka or Petersburg can be as low as $122 biennial. Motorcycles, trailers, and off-highway vehicles pay less; commercial and leased vehicles pay a 1.5x MVRT rate.

How fees are calculated in Alaska

  • State registration fee — flat by vehicle type: $100 passenger / pickup (2yr), $60 motorcycle, $30 trailer, $10 OHV, up to $300 tour bus.
  • MVRT — starts at a base by borough (about $150 in Anchorage / Mat-Su, $151 in Juneau, $221 in Unalaska) and depreciates roughly $16–$25 per model year, floored at $10–$19. Flat-rate communities charge $22 regardless of age.
  • EV / PHEV surcharge (HB 104) — $100 biennial for EVs, $50 for PHEVs, halved for annual terms.
  • Optional add-ons — $15 title, $15 lien, $10 walk-in fee, $25 Z-Tab, $2 emissions, plus specialty plate fees.

What affects your registration in Alaska?

  • Borough / city — MVRT rates and depreciation schedules differ.
  • Model year — older vehicles pay less MVRT (down to a per-borough minimum).
  • Vehicle category — passenger, motorcycle, trailer, motor home, taxicab, tour bus, farm, historic, APV, OHV.
  • Ownership — leased vehicles are classified commercial and pay 1.5x MVRT.
  • Fuel type — EVs, PHEVs, and alternative-fuel vehicles pay HB 104 surcharges.
  • Exemption status — Senior (65+), disability, disabled veteran, National Guard, Gold Star Family, and active-duty military waive registration and MVRT.
  • Service method — online / mail is free of surcharges; in-person adds $10.

Frequently asked questions

What factors affect vehicle registration fees in Alaska?

The main factors are your borough or city (which sets the MVRT rate), the vehicle's model year (older vehicles depreciate to a floor), the vehicle category, the fuel type (EVs and PHEVs pay HB 104 surcharges), and whether the vehicle is owned or leased (leased vehicles are commercial and pay 1.5x MVRT). Exemptions for seniors, disability, veterans, active-duty military, and Gold Star families waive most of the total.

Is this calculator accurate for all vehicles in Alaska?

It reflects 2025–2026 Alaska DMV schedules under AS 28.10.421 for the most common vehicle classes: passenger cars, motorcycles, trailers, motor homes, taxicabs, tour buses, farm and historic vehicles, and OHVs. IRP-apportioned commercial fleets and rare specialty exemptions may fall outside the standard formula — confirm those with the Alaska DMV.

Can I use this calculator for a new car and a used car in Alaska?

Yes. Alaska's MVRT depreciates from a base value each model year, so the calculator handles new and used vehicles identically — just pick the correct model year and borough / city. Vehicles 8+ years old also unlock the optional Z-Tab permanent registration.
