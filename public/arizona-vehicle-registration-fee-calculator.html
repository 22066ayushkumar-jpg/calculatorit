How much will my tag and registration cost in Arizona?

Most Arizona drivers pay a flat $8 registration fee plus the Vehicle License Tax (VLT). The VLT is the largest portion of your bill and is calculated using a strict depreciation formula rather than the current resale value. For a brand-new $35,000 car, expect to pay around $580 in VLT for the first year. For a 10-year-old vehicle originally priced at $25,000, the VLT drops to roughly $70. Since the state reduces the assessed value by 16.25% each year, your registration will always get cheaper the longer you own the vehicle.

How fees are calculated in Arizona

Arizona uses a specific mathematical formula to calculate the Vehicle License Tax (VLT) for standard passenger vehicles:

  • Base Value: The state first takes 60% of the manufacturer's base retail price (MSRP).
  • Depreciation: The assessed value drops by 16.25% for every year since the vehicle was first registered.
  • Tax Rate: New vehicles are charged $2.80 per $100 of assessed value. Used vehicles (previously registered by any owner) are charged $2.89 per $100 of assessed value.
  • Minimum VLT: The absolute minimum VLT applied by the state is $5.00.

What affects your registration in Arizona?

  • Original MSRP: The base retail price before options or destination charges drives the entire calculation.
  • Vehicle Age: The number of years since the vehicle was first registered anywhere.
  • New vs. Used Status: Determines if the tax multiplier is $2.80 (new) or $2.89 (used).
  • Alternative Fuel: Pure EVs and alternative fuel vehicles have dramatically different, often lower, assessment rates.

Frequently asked questions

How much is the average car registration in Arizona?

It varies widely based on MSRP and vehicle age, but a typical 5-year-old car ranges between $150 and $300, while new cars often range from $400 to $800+.

Is this calculator accurate for all vehicles in Arizona?

It is highly accurate for standard passenger vehicles and light trucks. However, alternative fuel vehicles (EVs), commercial trucks, and historic plates use entirely different fee structures.

How do I calculate the MSRP for registration fees in Arizona?

You don't calculate it yourself; the MVD uses the original base manufacturer's suggested retail price (MSRP) excluding options. If you don't know it, you can look up the base MSRP for your year/make/model online.

Can registration fees in Arizona change every year?

Yes. Your total fee decreases every year because the Vehicle License Tax (VLT) depreciates by 16.25% annually until it hits the $5 state minimum.

Can I use this calculator for a new car and a used car in Arizona?

Yes. Just select "New" or "Used" in the dropdown to automatically apply the correct tax rate ($2.80 per $100 for new, $2.89 per $100 for used).
