The Colorado vehicle registration fees calculator gives you a quick estimate of what the Colorado DMV will charge to register a car or light truck. Colorado is different from most states — your biggest line item is usually the Specific Ownership Tax (SOT), which is calculated from your vehicle's original MSRP and its age (not the price you actually paid). On top of that, Colorado adds a weight-based registration fee, FASTER Road and Bridge Safety surcharges, an EV or plug-in hybrid road-usage fee if applicable, emissions program fees in the Denver metro area, and the optional Keep Colorado Wild Pass. Use this Colorado vehicle registration fees calculator to preview those costs before you visit your county motor vehicle office.

How fees are calculated in Colorado

Colorado registration is built from several statutory pieces:

  • Specific Ownership Tax (C.R.S. 42-3-107) — 2.10% of the taxable value in year 1, stepping down to 1.50%, 1.20%, 0.90%, 0.45%, and a flat $3.00 after 10 years. Taxable value is 85% of MSRP for passenger cars and 75% for trucks.
  • Weight-based registration fee (C.R.S. 42-3-306) — increases with empty weight; trucks pay a higher schedule than passenger cars.
  • FASTER surcharges (C.R.S. 43-4-804 & 42-4-805) — Road Safety and Bridge Safety fees scaled by vehicle weight.
  • EV road-usage fee (SB21-260) — annual, inflation-adjusted fee for all-electric and plug-in hybrid vehicles.
  • Emissions, clerk hire, EMS, POST, insurance, and material fees — small mandatory line items on every registration.

What this calculator may not include

This estimator focuses on standard private-use registrations. It does not calculate Colorado state and local sales/use tax on the vehicle purchase (2.9% state plus local rates that can exceed 8% combined), late renewal penalties, personalized-plate premiums, specialty plate surcharges, county-specific processing fees, IRP apportioned commercial registrations, motor homes, trailers, or dealer plates. Always confirm the final total with your Colorado county motor vehicle office.

Frequently Asked Questions

What factors affect vehicle registration fees in Colorado?

Colorado ties fees to your original MSRP (via Specific Ownership Tax), the vehicle's age, its empty weight, fuel type (EVs and plug-in hybrids owe SB21-260 road-usage fees), whether you register in an emissions program area, and mandatory statewide surcharges like FASTER Road & Bridge Safety.

Is this calculator accurate for all vehicles in Colorado?

It covers most private passenger cars, SUVs, and light trucks up to 16,000 lbs using 2026 Colorado DMV rates. Commercial fleets, apportioned IRP registrations, motor homes, trailers, and specialty plates use different schedules and county charges may vary.

Can I use this calculator for a new car and a used car in Colorado?

Yes. Colorado's Specific Ownership Tax is always based on the vehicle's original MSRP and its age — not on your purchase price — so an older used car will pay a much lower ownership tax than a brand-new one with the same MSRP.
