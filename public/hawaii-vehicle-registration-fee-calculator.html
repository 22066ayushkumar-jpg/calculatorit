How much will my tag and registration cost in Hawaii?

Because Hawaii registration is determined almost entirely by the weight of your vehicle and the island you live on, the cost varies widely. For a typical mid-sized sedan weighing 3,300 lbs in Honolulu, expect to pay around $350 per year. For a heavier 4,500 lbs SUV in Maui, that cost can climb over $400. If you drive a fully electric vehicle, state (and sometimes county) surcharges are added to compensate for the lack of gas taxes.

How are fees calculated in Hawaii?

Hawaii uses a dual-tax system where both the state and your local county levy their own flat fees and weight-based taxes:

  • State Flat Fees: Every vehicle pays a $46.00 state registration fee, a $7.00 highway beautification fee, and a $0.50 emblem fee.
  • State Weight Tax: 1.75 cents per pound for vehicles up to 4,000 lbs; 2.00 cents per pound for vehicles 4,001 to 7,000 lbs; 2.25 cents per pound for 7,001 to 10,000 lbs.
  • County Flat Fees: Usually $20 (or $12 in Hawai?i County).
  • County Weight Tax: This is the largest variable. Honolulu charges 7 cents per pound, while Hawai?i County charges only 1.25 cents per pound.

What affects your registration in Hawaii?

  • Net Vehicle Weight: The heavier the car, the exponentially higher both the state and county taxes become.
  • County of Residence: Honolulu has the highest county weight tax for passenger cars. Hawai?i County (the Big Island) has the lowest.
  • Fuel Type: The state adds a flat $50 annual surcharge for EVs. Maui County adds an *additional* local EV surcharge of $100 (and $50 for standard hybrids).
  • Commercial vs. Passenger: Commercial freight vehicles have their own specific county weight tax multipliers.

What this calculator may not include

  • Safety Inspection Fees: Hawaii requires an annual safety check ($20-$25 paid directly to an authorized inspection station) before you can renew your tags.
  • Replacement costs for lost or damaged metal license plates.
  • State sales or use tax if you are importing a newly purchased vehicle from the mainland.

Frequently asked questions

How much is the average car registration in Hawaii?

The average car in Hawaii weighs between 3,000 and 4,000 pounds, resulting in an average total registration cost of $300 to $400 per year, depending heavily on the county you live in.

Is this calculator accurate for all vehicles in Hawaii?

This calculator is accurate for standard passenger cars, light trucks, SUVs, and commercial freight vehicles up to 10,000 lbs. Special vehicles, mopeds, trailers, and antique plates have different fee structures.

Can registration fees in Hawaii change every year?

Your fee will remain fairly consistent year after year unless the state legislature or your local county council votes to increase the weight tax multiplier per pound, or if you transfer the vehicle to a new owner.

Can I use this calculator for a new car and a used car in Hawaii?

Yes. Hawaii does not base registration on the vehicle's age, value, or MSRP. The registration fees for a brand-new 3,500 lb vehicle are exactly the same as they are for a 15-year-old 3,500 lb vehicle. If you are buying a used car, simply check the "Transfer Ownership" box to account for the title transfer fees.
