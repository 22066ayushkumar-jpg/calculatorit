The Utah vehicle registration fees calculator gives you a fast, mobile-friendly estimate of what you'll owe the Utah DMV in 2026. Utah uses a uniform age-based fee that decreases as your car gets older, layered on top of a fixed registration package ($75.50 for passenger cars, $73.00 for motorcycles), an alternative-fuel surcharge for hybrids and EVs, and a $10 corridor preservation fee in 11 counties. If you're buying a new or used vehicle, the calculator also adds Utah state and local sales tax (6.85%–9.05%), a $6 title fee, plate fee, and $2 per lien recording — everything you need to budget before you visit the DMV.

How fees are calculated in Utah

Utah stacks several fixed and variable charges. The uniform age fee is the biggest single line item and drops in tiers at 3, 6, 9, and 12 years of vehicle age. Registration components are flat and add to $75.50 (passenger) or $73.00 (motorcycle). Alt-fuel vehicles pay a flat annual surcharge that offsets lost gas-tax revenue. On a purchase, taxable amount = purchase price − trade-in − rebates, multiplied by your combined state + local rate.

What affects your registration in Utah?

  • Vehicle age. The uniform fee tier is the single biggest variable — a 2-year-old car pays $150, a 13-year-old car pays $10.
  • Fuel type. EVs add $180, PHEVs $64, and hybrids $25 annually.
  • County. Salt Lake, Utah, Davis, Weber, Box Elder, Tooele, Washington, Morgan, Rich, Wasatch, and one additional county add a $10 corridor fee.
  • Purchase price minus trade-in / rebates. Drives sales tax on new purchases.
  • Local sales tax rate. Ranges 6.85%–9.05% depending on city.
  • Plate, title, and lien selections for new registrations.

Frequently asked questions

Can registration fees in Utah change every year?

Yes. Your uniform age fee drops as your car crosses the 3-, 6-, 9-, and 12-year tiers, so the same vehicle costs less to register over time. The Utah Legislature can also adjust base rates — SB 91, effective 1/1/2026, raised the registration package to $75.50 for passenger cars. EV and PHEV fees are also indexed and can change annually.

How much does it cost to renew vehicle registration in Utah?

A typical passenger renewal is the $75.50 registration package plus the uniform age fee ($10–$150), any alt-fuel surcharge ($25 hybrid, $64 PHEV, $180 EV), and a $10 corridor fee in 11 counties. Most passenger renewals fall between $85 and $260. Use the calculator above with "Renewal Only" for your exact estimate.

Can I use this calculator for a new car and a used car in Utah?

Yes. Choose New Registration (Purchase) for either a brand-new or used vehicle purchase — the calculator adds Utah sales tax on (purchase price − trade-in − rebates), plus the $6 title, plate, and lien fees. Choose Renewal Only to skip purchase-side costs and estimate just the annual DMV renewal.
